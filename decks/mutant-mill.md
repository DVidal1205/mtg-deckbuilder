## Strategy

Mill our library (and our opponents' libraries) to stock graveyards with fatties, then use The Master's tap ability to pull the biggest threat of the turn onto the battlefield as a 3/3 green Mutant. **Master's text only strips "other" creature types and colors — all oracle abilities, ETB triggers, and static effects come along with the cheated body.** Keep milling, keep cheating, keep abusing the graveyard.

The deck fires five overlapping engines:

1. **Self-mill + opponent mill** — stock every graveyard so Master has targets. Bruvac the Grandiloquent doubles *opponent* mill specifically, letting us lock their libraries while ours fills at half pace.
2. **Rad counters** — opponents mill themselves every turn by just existing, stocking graveyards for Master to steal creatures out of.
3. **Yard tutoring + top-deck manipulation** — Buried Alive drops 3 creatures straight to yard for 3 mana. Brainstorm + fetchlands let us bury big creatures we've drawn into on top of the library for mill pickups. Entomb-style efficiency without needing Entomb itself.
4. **Mutate axis** — Brokkos lets us stack abilities onto Master (or any non-Human we control) by casting him for his mutate cost *from the graveyard.* This is how Master goes from 2/4 vanilla to trampling, stat-stacked, ability-layered monster.
5. **Backup reanimation** — Sheoldred, Whispering One (upkeep reanimate), Meren, Muldrotha, Animate Dead, Grave Researcher // Reanimate, and Buried Alive piles provide redundancy when Master is removed or taxed out. Mikaeus still gives undying to our non-Human Mutants for combat resilience.

Win conditions:

- **Twilight Diviner on a Master turn** — every Master activation creates a free token copy of the full printed creature (copy effects grab printed characteristics, not Master's 3/3 override). One activation on Craterhoof gives us a 3/3 Mutant AND a 5/5 Craterhoof token with ETB — doubling every reanimation.
- **Kalonian Hydra attack triggers** — with Mothman/Queen/Jenova/Frank Horrigan counter payloads, one Kalonian swing doubles everything. Follow up with Craterhoof Behemoth for lethal.
- **Sheoldred + Urborg** — Sheoldred keeps Swampwalk through Master's type strip; with Urborg making every land a Swamp, a Master-reanimated Sheoldred is unblockable while reanimating a creature every upkeep and forcing opp sacrifices every upkeep.
- **Syr Konrad + Mindcrank** — any damage to any player (combat, rad life loss, Konrad pings) mills that many cards from that player. Multiplayer tables mean someone takes damage most turns — watch for opp-yard hits on their turns, untap Master with Intruder Alarm, steal.
- **Mill drain finish** — Bruvac doubled Mesmeric Orb + Ruin Crab landfall + Icetill Explorer → opponents empty libraries over a few turn cycles.

## Key Synergies & Combo Lines

- **Marvin, Murderous Mimic + The Master** — Marvin has all activated abilities of other creatures we control. With Master out, Marvin *also* has "{T}: reanimate a creature milled this turn as a 3/3 Mutant." That's a second Master for 2 mana. Pair with Kiora's Follower (which Marvin also gains the untap ability of) and Seedborn Muse for 6-8 reanimations per round.
- **Twilight Diviner + Master (or any graveyard-enter)** — Master cheats a creature in as a 3/3 Mutant; Twilight Diviner sees "creature entered from graveyard" and creates a token copy of it. Copy effects read the card's *printed* characteristics, not Master's local 3/3 override — so the token enters at full stats (5/5 Craterhoof with ETB, 6/6 Grave Titan making tokens, 9/9 Terastodon destroying 3 things). Once per turn, but it fires on every reanimation turn.
- **Brokkos + The Master (or any non-Human)** — mutate Brokkos onto Master from the graveyard for {2}{U/B}{G}{G}. Combined creature keeps Master's tap-reanimation AND gains trample + the "may cast from graveyard via mutate" line. If Brokkos is on top, P/T becomes 6/6. If on bottom, Master stays 2/4 but we still get trample + graveyard recursion for Brokkos. Either way, Master is now much harder to remove permanently — graveyard → mutate → back on field.
- **The Master + Intruder Alarm + any mill engine** — Master cheats a creature in as a 3/3 Mutant → Intruder Alarm untaps all creatures (including Master) → tap Master again if we have mill output (Mesmeric Orb + our taps, Splinterfright's upkeep mill, Raul's activation). Chains until we run out of creature cards in graveyards. The deck's "oops we won" line.
- **The Master + Kiora's Follower + Seedborn Muse** — two untap vectors on Master. Kiora taps to untap Master for a second activation per turn, Seedborn Muse untaps on every opponent's turn. That's **four Master activations per round** minimum, more if Kiora's Follower is also untapped by Alarm.
- **Jenova, Ancient Calamity in a dying-Mutants deck** — Jenova draws a card equal to the power of every Mutant that dies on our turn. With Vannifar sacs, board wipes, and Master cycling Mutants, she turns combat trades into gas. She also adds +1/+1 counters equal to her power to another creature at beginning of combat, making that creature a Mutant — Coat of Arms and Kindred Discovery (Mutant) retargets.
- **Mikaeus, the Unhallowed + any free sac outlet** — we no longer run Altar; undying still makes our non-Human Mutants resilient in combat and punishes wraths that don't exile. Pair with Vannifar when we need a sorcery-speed sac for a tutor step.
- **Mindcrank + any damage** — Syr Konrad triggers, Glowing One combat damage (4 rad), rad mill life loss, opp-on-opp combat. Every point of life loss → that player mills that many. Intentional: multiplayer means someone takes damage most turns; opp-turn mills give Master a fresh "milled this turn" pool to answer on Intruder Alarm untaps.
- **Icetill Explorer + fetches** — extra land drop per turn + landfall mill + "may play lands from your graveyard." With four off-color fetches + Prismatic Vista, each land drop is a mill trigger for Ruin Crab and a self-mill for Icetill. Recycles lands when Mesmeric Orb puts them in the yard.
- **Vorinclex, Monstrous Raider — the counter multiplier** — every counter we put on anything is doubled. Rad counters given by Master, Scorchbeast, Wise Mothman, Glowing One, Raul, Jenova — all doubled. +1/+1 counters from Mothman mill, Mirelurk Queen mill, Kalonian Hydra ETB (4 → 8), Mikaeus undying (1 → 2), Agent Frank proliferate (each proliferated counter doubled too) — all doubled. He has **trample and haste** which Master's reanimated 3/3 keeps. He also cuts opposing counter strategies in half passively. Single largest force multiplier in the deck after Seedborn Muse.
- **Brainstorm + fetchland** — classic legacy trick. Brainstorm draws 3, put two junk cards back on top, crack a fetch to shuffle them away. In our deck, we can *choose* to put big creatures back on top, then mill them via any of our 10+ mill engines. Acts as both smoothing and setup for Master targets.
- **Buried Alive for 3 mana** — dump three fatties directly to yard (e.g. Craterhoof + Terastodon + Hornet Queen). Next turns: Master + Diviner + Meren/Muldrotha/Sheoldred chew through the pile.
- **Kalonian Hydra + any +1/+1 counter board** — Mothman puts counters on mill, Mirelurk Queen grows herself on mill, Agent Frank proliferates twice per attack. Kalonian Hydra attacks → **double all +1/+1 counters on every creature we control.** Even reanimated at 3/3, Kalonian still ETBs with 4 counters (replacement effect still applies), and the attack trigger is what wins games.
- **Raul, Trouble Shooter + any mill** — Raul lets us cast a spell from among cards milled this turn, once per turn. Reanimation vector #2, and works for sorceries/enchantments — mill Living Death with Raul in play, cast it for free.
- **Agent Frank Horrigan proliferate** — proliferate twice on ETB and every attack. Doubles rad output, multiplies +1/+1 counters on Mothman/Queen, adds loyalty to Master's "rad counters on a chosen player" plan. Indestructible as long as he attacked.
- **Terastodon via Master** — cheat Terastodon in as a 3/3. ETB still destroys up to 3 noncreature permanents. Rhystic Study, Smothering Tithe, fast mana, game changers all gone for the cost of one Master activation. Opponents get 3/3 Elephant tokens — sure, but they just ate three key pieces.
- **Hornet Queen + Coat of Arms** — Queen brings 4 1/1 deathtouch flyer tokens. With Coat of Arms active, every Insect pumps every other Insect — and every Mutant pumps every other Mutant. Board becomes a hornet + mutant army that can't be blocked safely.
- **Prime Speaker Vannifar** — sorcery-speed creature tutor: sac, get a creature with +1 MV directly onto the battlefield. Ladder: Ruin Crab (1) → Splinterfright (3) → Sidisi (4) → Wise Mothman (4) → Mirelurk Queen (5) → Sheoldred (7) → Craterhoof (8). Combined with Meren/Muldrotha we recur sac fodder.
- **Bruvac the Grandiloquent** — "If an opponent would mill, they mill twice that many." Mesmeric Orb, Ruin Crab, rad, Kitsune's Technique all become asymmetric obliteration. Opponents hit empty libraries fast while we still stock our own yard at the normal rate.
- **Soul-Guide Lantern** — ETB exile one card from a yard; sac to exile all opponents' graveyards (feeds our "don't empower opp reanimator" plan) or pay {1} to sac and draw when we're flooding.

## Game Changers (3 / 3 used, B3 compliant)

- **Rhystic Study** — scales with multiplayer and we usually have mana open at 3+ players.
- **Cyclonic Rift** — asymmetric bounce wipe. Resets opposing boards without touching our Mutants.
- **Seedborn Muse** — 4x Master activations per round. Single largest individual-card multiplier in the deck.

## Notes

- **Tutoring is intentional and minimal.** Prime Speaker Vannifar is our *only* tutor, and she's a recurring engine that requires sac fodder — consistent with B3 "find a piece, not the combo."
- **Removal suite:** Abrupt Decay (uncounterable MV≤3), Assassin's Trophy (any permanent), Drown in the Loch (modal counter/kill that scales with opp yard size), Toxic Deluge (sized wipe), Cyclonic Rift (asymmetric bounce). Terastodon is backup wipe-on-a-stick.
- **Sac outlets:** Prime Speaker Vannifar (sorcery-speed, tutors). No free sac outlet — we traded Altar + Protean Hulk for Sheoldred engine + yard hate.
- **Protection:** Lightning Greaves (pseudo-hexproof on Master or a threat), Swiftfoot Boots (hexproof + haste), Heroic Intervention (wrath-proofer for our go-wide turns).
- **Mana base:** 36 lands. 4 fetches (Misty, Verdant, Polluted, Prismatic Vista) + 3 OG duals + 3 shocks + 3 bond lands (Morphic Pool, Undergrowth Stadium, Rejuvenating Springs) + Zagoth Triome + Urborg + Yavimaya + 7 utility + 13 basics (7 Forest / 5 Swamp / 1 Island). Urborg + Yavimaya make every land also tap for B and G once both are out. ETB tapped: Zagoth + Bojuka Bog only.
- **Why Hornet Queen over Vanquisher's Banner:** go-wide bodies with deathtouch/flying are harder for opponents to race through, and they give Vannifar sac fodder.
- **Why Splinterfright despite losing his scaling on reanimation:** hard-cast at 2G he's a 20+/20+ trampler by turn 6 in this deck. If he does get milled and reanimated at 3/3, we still benefit from his upkeep mill ability.
- **Why Mikaeus (BBB triple-pip) is worth the fixing cost:** undying on every non-Human Mutant we control makes our 3/3s resilient and grows them after each death. With Vorinclex, undying returns with two +1/+1 counters.
- **Why Marvin (colorless 2-drop) over a traditional creature:** being colorless means Master-reanimated-Marvin is still a Mutant Toy, and he picks up every activated ability on the field. In the lategame with 5+ creatures on board, Marvin has 8+ activated abilities — tap for mill, tap for reanimate, tap for untap, sac for land, etc. Untyped swiss-army body.
- **Why Brainstorm with fetches?** Fetches shuffle, Buried Alive shuffles, Prime Speaker Vannifar shuffles. With self-mill engines, we don't need to shuffle away bad Brainstorm picks — we can *mill them away* instead. Brainstorm becomes "look at 3 cards, keep the best, seed the yard with the rest."
- **Flex slots to consider swapping:** Assassin's Trophy → Dismember, Stitcher's Supplier → Chthonian Nightmare for more reanimation density, Abrupt Decay → Feed the Swarm.
